Legislative and Public Policy Analysis

Research and Policy Development

I conducted legal and policy analysis on more than 15 legislative reforms in Egypt. My research addressed legislative development, constitutional amendments, parliamentary performance, election law, investment law, public accountability, and institutional reform.

I produced and supervised analytical reports used in discussions with civil society organizations, researchers, international organizations, and other stakeholders interested in Egyptian legal and political development.

My research experience includes both qualitative and quantitative methods, policy evaluation, legal document review, report writing, focus groups, survey research, and the preparation of strategic recommendations.

Research Areas: Legislative reform, constitutional studies, and governance

Methods: Legal analysis, document review, focus groups, and survey research

Outputs: Policy papers, analytical reports, and legislative summaries

Parliamentary Performance Monitoring

Eight Years of Parliamentary Research

Over a period of approximately eight years, I participated in projects that evaluated parliamentary performance through draft legislation, committee activity, public reports, and institutional analysis.

I produced monthly analytical reports on the performance of the Shura Council, including six reports in 2013. I also supervised research concerning the Committee of Fifty for Constitutional Amendments during 2013 and 2014.

My work also included reports on the Unified Investment Law, the Legislative Reform Committee, neglected parliamentary issues, and the relationship between legislative institutions and public accountability.

Duration: Approximately eight years

Activities: Monitoring, legal research, report writing, and legislative evaluation

Institutions Studied: Parliament, Shura Council, constitutional committees, and reform bodies

Public Education and Participation

Programs: Voter education, Democratic Schools, and community seminars
