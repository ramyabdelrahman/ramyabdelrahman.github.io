My Project Experience

My projects reflect more than ten years of experience in legal research, parliamentary monitoring, legislative reform, civic education, voter awareness, transparency, anti-corruption, community engagement, program management, and public communication.

These projects allowed me to combine my legal education with practical leadership. My responsibilities included developing project ideas, conducting research, preparing reports, coordinating teams, communicating with partners, organizing public activities, delivering training, and presenting complex information to community audiences.

The projects also demonstrate the personal connection between my professional experience and my long-term interest in law, history, public policy, democratic institutions, and public service.

Project Areas: Law, policy, governance, elections, education, and community engagement

My Roles: Researcher, program leader, trainer, writer, and project coordinator

Primary Skills: Research, leadership, writing, training, and public communication

Geographic Focus: Egypt and local communities

Parliamentary Performance Monitoring

Eight Years of Legislative Monitoring

Parliamentary performance monitoring was one of the longest and most important areas of my professional work. Over approximately eight years, I participated in projects that evaluated parliamentary performance through draft legislation, committee activity, parliamentary reports, and institutional analysis.

I examined the relationship between proposed laws, parliamentary performance, government policy, and the public interest. This required reviewing documents, identifying important legislative developments, and presenting findings in clear analytical reports.

My Role and Contribution

  • Researched draft legislation and parliamentary activity.
  • Evaluated the performance of legislative institutions.
  • Prepared and supervised analytical reports.
  • Identified institutional and legislative concerns.
  • Connected parliamentary research with public policy and accountability.

Duration: Approximately eight years

Research: Draft laws, reports, committees, and parliamentary performance

Outputs: Analytical reports and legislative summaries

Shura Council Analytical Reports

Monthly Parliamentary Analysis

In 2013, I produced six monthly analytical reports examining the performance of Egypt’s Shura Council. The reports evaluated legislative activities, institutional performance, and the relationship between parliamentary work and broader public issues.

This project required continuous monitoring, document review, legal and policy analysis, report writing, and the ability to organize large amounts of information into clear findings.

My Role and Contribution

  • Monitored Shura Council activities and performance.
  • Reviewed legislative and institutional documents.
  • Produced six analytical reports during 2013.
  • Explained legislative developments in accessible language.
  • Connected legal research with policy evaluation.

Year: 2013

Reports: Six monthly analytical reports

Focus: Shura Council performance and legislative activity

Constitutional and Legislative Reform Research

Committee of Fifty and Legislative Reform

I supervised and contributed to reports concerning the Committee of Fifty for Constitutional Amendments during 2013 and 2014. This work examined constitutional development, institutional design, public participation, and the possible legal and political effects of proposed amendments.

I also prepared and contributed to reports concerning the Unified Investment Law and the Legislative Reform Committee during 2014 and 2015.

Across my legal and policy work, I conducted analysis concerning more than 15 legislative reforms in Egypt.

My Role and Contribution

  • Reviewed constitutional and legislative proposals.
  • Supervised research and analytical reporting.
  • Compared legal proposals and institutional alternatives.
  • Evaluated possible effects on government and society.
  • Prepared findings for professional and public audiences.

Constitutional Research: Committee of Fifty, 2013–2014

Legislative Research: Unified Investment Law and Legislative Reform Committee

Overall Analysis: More than 15 legislative reforms

“Ally Sotak” Voter Education Campaign

Raise Your Voice

“Ally Sotak,” meaning “Raise Your Voice,” was a two-year voter education campaign designed to help citizens understand Egypt’s electoral systems and participate with greater knowledge and confidence.

The campaign addressed parliamentary elections, including the People’s Assembly and Shura Council, presidential elections, and constitutional referendums.

This project was personally meaningful to me because it connected legal knowledge with direct community service. It allowed me to speak with citizens, answer questions, explain voting procedures, and make complex electoral information more accessible.

My Role and Contribution

  • Helped design and coordinate voter education activities.
  • Explained electoral laws and voting procedures.
  • Participated in public seminars and community meetings.
  • Prepared and communicated educational information.
  • Encouraged informed and responsible civic participation.

Duration: Two years

Focus: Voter education and electoral awareness

Elections: Parliamentary, presidential, and constitutional referendums

Recognition: Honorary shield for leadership in civic education

Democratic Schools Project

Student Leadership and School Elections

The Democratic Schools Project was a civic education initiative designed to teach middle and high school students about student union elections, participation, leadership, representation, and democratic procedures.

Phase Two of the project continued for approximately two years and reached students in five governorates. The project connected democratic principles with an environment students understood directly: their schools.

This project connected with my own early experience as a class leader. As a student, I had learned that leadership could begin with identifying a shared problem and organizing classmates to improve their environment.

My Role and Contribution

  • Supported program planning and implementation.
  • Helped prepare civic education and election materials.
  • Coordinated training activities and participants.
  • Connected democratic concepts with student experiences.
  • Supported activities across five governorates.

Duration: Two years during Phase Two

Audience: Middle and high school students

Geographic Reach: Five governorates

Focus: Student union elections and youth leadership

King’s Witness Project

Transparency and Anti-Corruption

The King’s Witness Project focused on supporting witnesses and whistleblowers who reported corruption cases involving government institutions.

The project addressed transparency, accountability, institutional integrity, and the challenges faced by individuals attempting to report misconduct.

This project strengthened my understanding of how legal systems, public institutions, civil society, and individual courage interact in efforts to challenge corruption.

My Role and Contribution

  • Supported project activities involving witnesses and whistleblowers.
  • Contributed to research and documentation.
  • Helped connect individual cases with institutional concerns.
  • Supported public awareness of transparency and accountability.
  • Applied legal research and organizational skills.

Duration: One year

Focus: Witnesses, whistleblowers, and corruption reporting

Areas: Transparency, accountability, and institutional integrity

Citizen’s Box Project

Monitoring Local Councils

The Citizen’s Box Project focused on monitoring local councils and examining their performance, responsiveness, and relationship with citizens.

The project considered how local institutions implemented public responsibilities and how citizens’ experiences could be used to identify problems and improve local governance.

The project produced recommendations intended to strengthen implementation, accountability, and communication between local councils and communities.

My Role and Contribution

  • Helped monitor local council performance.
  • Collected and organized information about implementation.
  • Identified problems affecting local communities.
  • Contributed to recommendations for institutional improvement.
  • Connected policy analysis with local experiences.

Year: 2013

Focus: Local council monitoring

Outputs: Analysis and recommendations

Goal: Improved implementation and accountability

Parliament Neglected Issues Project

Reconsidering Parliamentary Reports

The Parliament Neglected Issues Project revisited parliamentary reports and public issues that had not received sufficient legislative attention or discussion.

The project examined how important reports and recommendations could be overlooked and considered ways to return those issues to public and institutional discussion.

Duration: One year

Research: Parliamentary reports and neglected issues

Goal: Renewed discussion and public accountability
