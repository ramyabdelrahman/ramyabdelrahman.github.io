Legal Research, Public Policy, and Civic Leadership

Connecting Legal Experience, Historical Study, and Public Service

I am a senior History student at George Mason University preparing for law school and a future legal career in the United States. I hold a Bachelor of Laws from Helwan University and an Associate of Arts and Sciences from Germanna Community College, where I graduated Summa Cum Laude.

My background includes more than ten years of experience in legal research, legislative reform, public policy, civic education, and civil society leadership in Egypt.

Professional Profile

My professional experience combines legal research, policy analysis, organizational leadership, program development, grant writing, and public engagement. I have researched legislative and constitutional reforms, prepared legal and policy reports, managed civic education programs, coordinated training activities, and worked with local and international organizations.

From 2009 to 2015, I served as Executive Director of the Egyptian Democratic Institute. In this position, I supervised research projects, civic education programs, voter awareness activities, training initiatives, and community-based projects.

Selected Professional Impact

  • Conducted legal and policy analysis on more than 15 legislative reforms in Egypt.
  • Prepared reports on parliamentary performance, constitutional amendments, election law, and legislative development.
  • Contributed to parliamentary monitoring projects over a period of eight years.
  • Managed civic education, voter awareness, and community engagement programs.
  • Supported student civic education activities across five governorates.
  • Contributed to transparency, accountability, and anti-corruption initiatives.
  • Developed professional experience in program management, grant writing, research, and training.
